Des nouvelles dalbion (News from Albion)

Sil vous en plaist escouter (Listen if you please)

Mon frere et mon cōpaignō (My brother and my companion)

Saichez qua mon retourner (Know that when I return)

Iay este deca la mer (I've been there, from the sea,)

Receu a joyeuse chiere (Received so cheerfully)

The Traitor's Gate is visible in the foreground. This was the an entrance through which many prisoners were brought into the Tower of London. Prisoners would be brought under London Bridge, passing beneath the heads of the recently beheaded.

This is the herald of Edward IV, who was the likely recipient of the manuscript. Edward died suddenly in 1483, and production of the manuscript halted abruptly.
